Abstract

Multi-step motion prediction for continuum robots is difficult, especially under actuation distribution shift, where error accumulation can distort the predicted steady response and destabilize rollouts. This paper introduces a hybrid equilibrium-anchored residual-learning framework for a tendon-driven 3D continuum arm that makes steady behavior explicit. An equilibrium prior is learned from inexpensive static equilibrium data and used in a contractive update that continuously pulls predictions toward the equilibrium estimate, improving rollout stability. A lightweight feature-lifted residual model, linear in parameters, learns the remaining one-step mismatch from dynamic trajectory data, recovering transient dynamics. The approach is validated on 200-step simulation rollouts under stronger and faster actuation than in training, with an additional soft-tail hardware test under actuation-frequency shift. The Hybrid method reduces backbone position RMSE by 26% and tip position RMSE by 27%, producing consistent accuracy gains over prior-only and residual-only predictors while remaining stable across all tested trajectories. The same proposed model also improves robustness on standard nonlinear benchmarks against a combined Koopman baseline under matched evaluation protocols.

Methodology

Our approach leverages an equilibrium prior learned from inexpensive static data to anchor dynamic predictions. This acts as a stabilizing reference that suppresses long-term drift. We then learn a residual correction from transient dynamic data to capture the missing high-frequency dynamics. By separating the steady-state from the transient mismatch, our model maintains stability even when rolled out for hundreds of steps under actuation shifts.

Equilibrium-anchored residual learning: (a) The equilibrium prior is learned from static data and anchors the intermediate state. A residual then learns the remaining one-step mismatch. (b) During OOD rollouts, this two-step recursion drastically reduces error accumulation.

Setup & Simulation

Tendon-driven continuum arm: The model includes three routing tendons and spacer disks. Actuation commands induce complex 3D bending dynamics.

Rollout Stability under Actuation Shift

Under out-of-distribution actuation (larger amplitudes and higher frequencies), naive residual methods drift and eventually blow up. The pure equilibrium prior remains stable but exhibits large steady-state errors. The Hybrid approach effectively combines the stability of the prior with the accuracy of the residual.

Rollout Error: Our Hybrid model substantially reduces the median shape RMSE over time and tightens the error distribution across unseen test trajectories.
Backbone Configuration: Over a 6s rollout, the Hybrid model maintains tight adherence to the ground truth backbone, preventing the catastrophic drift seen in unanchored residual approaches.

Hardware Validation & Benchmarks

Beyond simulation, we validate our method on a physical PneuNet soft tail and standard nonlinear benchmarks (Van der Pol oscillator and Pendulum-Cart).

Soft-Tail Hardware & Van der Pol: The Hybrid model significantly limits tracking error under physical actuation-frequency shifts. Furthermore, compared to state-of-the-art residual Koopman baselines, the anchored formulation achieves a 0% catastrophic failure rate on extended non-linear benchmark rollouts.
