Stabilizing 3D Continuum-Arm Rollouts

Equilibrium anchoring plus residual learning for stable long-horizon prediction under actuation shift.

Ahsan Tanveer, Rahdar Hussain Afridi, Waqar Hussain Afridi, Feitian Zhang, Guangming Xie

Artifact GitHub Video Citation
Idea

Keep a steady-state reference inside the recursive predictor.

Problem

Open-loop continuum-arm rollouts drift when test actuation is stronger or faster than training actuation.

Anchor

Static equilibrium data learns the command-conditioned steady shape used at every rollout step.

Residual

A lightweight linear residual learns transient mismatch around the anchored prediction.

Setup

Three tendon commands, 144-dimensional backbone state, 200 rollout steps.

Results

Hybrid rollouts reduce distal drift while staying stable.

26%

Backbone RMSE reduction

Relative to the equilibrium-only anchor.

27%

Tip RMSE reduction

Over 200-step out-of-distribution rollouts.

51%

Hardware gain

Minimum reduction over anchor-only soft-tail prediction.

0%

Catastrophic rate

For the reported Van der Pol hybrid runs.
